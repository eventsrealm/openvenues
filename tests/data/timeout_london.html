Time Out says

Posted: Thu Dec 11 2014

This Brixton live music venue and bar boasts a bit of a reputation for the diversity of its programme, with everything from roots and reggae to hip hop, underground Latin, European gypsy folk and ska. There are comedy nights, too. 

In addition to the music and live events, the street food coming from the kitchen does pretty brisk business. Three outfits - El Chingo Mexican Grill, Alchino Pizzeria and Tony's - showcase their wares, so expect tacos, burritos and quesedillas; wood-fired pizzas; jerk chicken, ackee and saltfish and plenty of plantain from Tony's. 

Hootananny shows many of the big football matches, as well as some other sports.

Hootananny says

Hootananny Brixton brings the festival vibes to London in an enormous Victorian award winning pub music venue with the best bands. The atmosphere is great for dancing with a packed area below the stage. Reviewers say time and again it is inclusive and friendly, with a gigantic beer garden, tented and warm in the winter, with 3 pool tables, pop up food: mouth watering tacos, jerk chicken and oven fired pizza and a new cocktail bar. Renowned for live hiphop, dubstep folk, roots reggae (a speciality) blues, ska, jazz-funk, battle rap, gypsy swing, African high-life and flamenco rock, party goers come in their droves. Live Comedy on Fridays, with Burlesque dancers as well as 3 live bands and DJs kicks the weekend off in style. 3 bands of Dancehall, electro rock and pop with fat hooks and bumping soundsysems and DJs continue the weekend on Saturday night, with our global roots world music on Sunday evening. Live music 5 nights a week, this pub is an award winning Music pub, winning The Great British Pub Awards for Best Live Music Pub UK and The London Life Style Awards for Best Music Venue in London.
