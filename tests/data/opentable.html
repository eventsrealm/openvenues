About Beast

5425 NE 30th Ave
Portland, OR 97211
View Larger Map

BEAST features the bounty of the Northwest with French inspiration in mind. A six-course prix-fixe dinner is offered five nights a week in a communal setting. The menu changes Wednesday and remains the same for the rest of the week based upon the ingredients of the season. A four-course prix-fixe brunch is offered every Sunday.

Wine pairings are available for dinner and brunch. As our menu changes weekly, so do they.

We politely decline substitutions as we offer a set menu only.

A credit card is required to secure all reservations. We have a 24-hour cancellation policy for parties up to five people and a 48-hour cancellation policy for parties of six or more. Any cancellations within the aforementioned timeframes will result in an $85 charge per person and $40 for brunch.

Good Foord and a Fine Evening!

PigglyWigglyHiggly
Dined on November 23, 2014

Foodies who enjoy communal dining (and are lucky, as we were, to have companionable diners at our end of the table), will fInd Beast a treat. While we prefer non-communal dining, Beast's service was pleasant, non-intrusive and efficient; and, the size of the place (it seats only about 25-30 people) almost mandates the communal format as well as the "be-on-time-or-we start-without-you," "no-substitutions-nearly-unalterable" six-course, prix fixe menu (tip included) that comes with or without wine pairings. We chose "with," and wished afterward that we had not. The pairings offered interesting wines, but for at least two courses we found the pairings mismatched. It is difficult, champagne excepted, to find a single pairing for a charcuterie plate of bite-size portions of steak tartare, foie gras bon-bon with peanut shortbread, chicken-liver mousse, pate forestier, crispy pork shoulder and belly rillettes, and pork sausage. This was paired with a well-crafted Willamette Valley pinot blanc, which meant the wine was overwhelmed by the charcuterie, which it did not enhance. A better choice, among many alternatives, would have been a second pour of the Loire sparkler that accompanied the superb cauliflower veloute that began the meal. Dinner was capped off with an excellent dessert: a cardamom chilled cream and lemon curd parfait with whipped creme fraiche, served with an espresso cocoa nib tuile and perfectly paired with a nice cognac. Beast is a $20-25 ride from downtown Portland, and not wanting to be late we followed Beast's instructions, arriving early to have drinks at its establishment, the bar Expatriate, located across the street. This place knows its drinks: top-shelf (and some hard to find liquors), knowledgeable mixologists and very hip ambiance, including an fine and uncommon back bar. There's just enough buzz to indicate patrons are having a good time, and just enough quiet to allow intimate conversation.
